Part 03

The Perceptron

1958 — Born Too Early

Frank Rosenblatt was a Cornell psychologist. In 1958, he built the Mark I Perceptron — a machine that could look at shapes through a camera and learn to identify them. Not by rules. By experience.

In 1969, Marvin Minsky co-authored Perceptrons, proving that a single-layer perceptron could not solve XOR — a simple logical problem. The field concluded: neural networks are a dead end. Funding evaporated.
