2012 — The Big Bang

ImageNet

The task: identify objects in 1.2 million photographs across 1,000 categories. For decades, the best algorithms hit a ceiling of 26% error.

Geoffrey Hinton's graduate students — Alex Krizhevsky and Ilya Sutskever — submitted a deep neural network called AlexNet.

15.3%AlexNet Error
26.2%2nd Place

The gap was so large the field didn't believe it. Then they checked the code. It was just a bigger neural network, trained on GPUs.
The deep learning era had begun.
