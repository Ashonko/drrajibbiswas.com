About

Since 2019, I have been working on the Child Health and Mortality Prevention Surveillance (CHAMPS). CHAMPS is a global surveillance network that generates and shares accurate information on causes of stillbirths and child deaths in high-mortality settings in Africa and South Asia.

Working closely with communities and their leaders, CHAMPS teams work quickly to identify deaths and with a family's consent, uses innovative diagnostic methods including Minimally Invasive Tissue Sampling (MITS)1 to investigate the causes.

The network operates in 22 catchment areas across 9 countries, providing trusted, evidence-based data on child mortality to inform policy, public health practice, and health system changes.

The Bangladesh site, where I am working, operates in the Baliakandi sub-district of Rajbari district, a region with a high child mortality rate. CHAMPS Bangladesh covers a population of over 216,000 people under surveillance and works with key facilities including the Baliakandi Upazila Health Complex (BUHC), Faridpur Medical College Hospital (FMCH), and Zahid Memorial Children Hospital (ZMCH). The site is implemented through collaboration between icddr,b, IEDCR, and BSMMU, representing Bangladesh as the initial selection from South East Asia in the CHAMPS network.

Data to action and other research activities

Over the years, I have been the key implementer of the CHAMPS data-to-action activities at the facility level. These activities include addressing major causes of neonatal mortalities, such as sepsis. We conduct regular hospital environmental sampling to pinpoint the sources and understand the transmission dynamics of pathogens that may cause nosocomial infections. One of our research initiatives led to the introduction of separate suction tubes for each admitted child at ZMCH. Another finding, highlighting the lack of adequate clinical information, led to the introduction of a structured clinical form at FMCH.
